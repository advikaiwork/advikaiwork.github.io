What I Learned from My First NCFC Classic Tryout

October 28, 2025 · 6 min read

I tried out at the South Side Soccer Fields for my first NCFC Classic tryout, and honestly, I didn’t know what to expect. I had heard about how competitive NCFC was and how many talented players came to these tryouts, but it still felt totally different when I actually got there. There were players from all over — from different schools, towns, and even clubs — all warming up, stretching, and juggling soccer balls in the cool morning air. I immediately noticed how many different skill levels there were. Some players looked like they had been playing forever, with sharp passes, quick touches, and confidence in everything they did. Others were still learning, missing a few touches here and there, but still giving full effort.

What caught my attention the most was how the fields were arranged. The top fields had players who seemed more advanced, while the bottom fields had players who were still developing. At first, it was a little intimidating to see that separation, almost like a ranking, but as the tryout went on, I realized it wasn’t about putting anyone down—it was about letting coaches see everyone fairly. The coaches moved players around between fields, and it showed that where you start doesn’t decide where you’ll finish. That really stuck with me because it meant everyone had a chance to prove themselves no matter what.

The coaches were really good too. They were organized, clear, and encouraging. They explained each drill in detail, told us what they were looking for, and made sure we understood how to show our best. Even when someone messed up a pass or missed a shot, they didn’t yell or embarrass anyone—they just encouraged us to keep going. I realized that coaches pay attention to much more than just skill; they’re watching for attitude, effort, and teamwork. The players who listened, stayed positive, and supported others always stood out.

Tryouts can be stressful because you want to impress the coaches and play well, but I learned that the pressure only gets worse if you focus too much on being perfect. At first, I was nervous and made a few bad passes, but then I started to relax and just play soccer the way I normally do. Once I stopped worrying about what field I was on or who was watching, I started to play better—communicating more, moving faster, and enjoying the game.

I also learned how important fitness is. The tryout was fast-paced, with lots of movement and short breaks. You had to stay sharp the whole time. It showed me that being technically good isn’t enough—you need endurance to keep your level up from start to finish. I promised myself after that day that I’d work more on running, agility, and conditioning.

Another thing I noticed was that teamwork stood out more than fancy moves. Some players tried to do everything themselves, but the coaches seemed more impressed with players who passed, talked, and helped their teammates look good. One time I made a simple pass that led to a goal, and I saw a coach give me a quick nod—that moment taught me that simple, smart soccer matters more than flashy plays.

By the end of the tryout, everyone was tired but smiling. Whether people made the team or not, everyone learned something. For me, the biggest lesson was that tryouts are about growth, not just results. Even if you don’t make it this time, you walk away with more experience and motivation. Soccer isn’t just about being the best; it’s about improving every time you step on the field.

Looking back now, I’m grateful for that day at the South Side Soccer Fields because it showed me that hard work, effort, and attitude can take you further than talent alone. If I had to give advice to anyone trying out next year, I’d say: don’t overthink it. Be confident, listen to your coaches, work hard, stay positive, and most of all—enjoy the game. Because when you love what you’re doing, everything else falls into place.
